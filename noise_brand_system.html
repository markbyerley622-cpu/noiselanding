00 — WHAT CHANGED IN v2

Purple, made intentional.

v2 promotes the violet identity from the v3 product UI into the official system. The rule that governs everything below: purple is the interaction color, never the dominant one. ~85% of every surface stays neutral charcoal and black; purple is rationed to actions, focus, active states, links, data, and rare hero moments. Nothing about the original maturity — the quiet dark, the waveform mark, the orb, Space Grotesk × Inter — was thrown away.

Anchored on #8B5CF6
The exact violet from the product UI becomes purple-500 — the core of a full 10-step scale.
Gradient demoted, blue kept
The signal gradient now spans the full blue→indigo→violet→magenta spectrum — the v1 blue lives on in the orb. Reserved for hero/brand moments only.
Roles over swatches
Components reference --accent, --focus, --link — not raw hex. Recolor once, everywhere updates.

08 — MOTION PRINCIPLES

One living mark, calm transitions.

The orb is "Noise Brain" — an emissive light source, not a painted disc. It breathes slowly (~6s). One easing family, slightly overdamped — nothing snaps or bounces.

The orbRadial gradient + soft bloom, brightening to a near-white core.
Ambient glowBackground bloom behind hero & CTA moments. Low opacity, large blur.
Mini orbThe AI's presence in-product — assistant avatar, "thinking" state.
Calm by defaultEasing var(--ease) cubic-bezier(.2,.8,.2,1). Durations 120–320ms for UI, 600ms for entrances.
Chaos → clarityTransitions resolve scattered elements into order — the brand idea in motion.
09 — PURPLE ACCENT SYSTEM

Noise signature effects.

The 80/20 rule made literal: keep ~85% of the interface neutral, spend purple only where it does work. Every effect below ships with a usage rule — restraint is the brand.

→
Purple glow3 tiers (sm/md/lg). Hover lift on CTAs, premium cards, the AI send-button. One glow per viewport — never decorative.
Glassrgba surface + blur(8–16px) + .12 hairline. Toolbars, popovers, modals over content. Not on flat backgrounds.
Gradientindigo→violet→magenta. Hero headlines, orb, wordmark dot, premium edges only. Max one moment per screen.
Focus ring0 0 0 3px rgba(139,92,246,.5). Identical on every focusable element. Always visible — never removed for aesthetics.
Neutral shadowDefault for depth — colourless. --sh-1/2/3. Use purple glow only to signal intelligence or premium.
Feature top-glowFaint radial from the top edge = "AI did something here." Cards, banners, KPI alerts.
10 — ACCESSIBILITY

Premium and legible.

Purple's biggest risk is low contrast on dark. The rule: purple-500 is for fills and borders, not text. For text and icons on dark, step up to purple-300/400. Targets are WCAG AA.

Contrast — purple text on base #08080C

purple-500 body text3.6 · fail body
purple-400 — links5.9 · AA
purple-300 — emphasis9.1 · AAA
primary text --t117 · AAA

Rules

✓Use purple-400+ for any text or icon that must be read on dark.
✓White text (#fff) on solid purple-500/600 fills — 4.6:1, passes.
✓Keep the focus ring visible on every control; pair colour with shape/icon for status.
✕purple-500 as body or label text on a dark surface.
✕Conveying state by colour alone — always add a label, dot or icon.
✕Targets under 44×44px or motion without a reduced-motion fallback.
