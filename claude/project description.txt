  What Noise Actually Is

  Noise is a memory and intelligence layer that sits on top of every channel you communicate through. It connects to all
  your messaging, email, social, calendar, and file channels, normalizes everything into one model, and then builds a
  brain on top: it remembers every relationship, scores its health, forecasts decay, surfaces what needs you, and drafts
  replies in your voice.

  The critical positioning distinction you asked for:

  ▎ It is NOT a unified inbox. A unified inbox just piles your messages into one list. Noise integrates every channel as
  ▎ a substrate so it can do the intelligent layer on top — memory, relationship health, forecasting, action. The
  ▎ integration is the foundation; the memory + intelligence is the product.

  ⚠️ One thing to fix: your current README.md literally calls it an "AI-powered unified inbox." That contradicts the
  positioning you want. Don't pull marketing copy from the README — it's the old framing. Lead with memory +
  intelligence, treat channels as the foundation that makes it possible.

  The Three Layers (your core narrative)

  This is the spine the whole landing page should hang on:

  1. The Integration Layer — "Every channel, one substrate"

  Noise connects to 14+ live channels end-to-end, normalizing them all into one UniversalMessage model. This isn't a
  list of logos for show — every new channel instantly inherits identity resolution, memory, health scoring, and actions
  with zero extra work. This is the foundation, not the pitch.

  Fully wired today:
  - Email: Gmail, Outlook, any IMAP/SMTP server
  - Messaging: Slack, Microsoft Teams, Discord, Telegram, WhatsApp (via Twilio), SMS
  - Social DMs: Instagram, Facebook Messenger
  - Calendar: Google Calendar, Outlook Calendar
  - Files: Google Drive, Dropbox
  - CRM/Tasks: Salesforce, ClickUp

  (Stubbed/roadmap, declared but not built: iMessage, LinkedIn, X, TikTok, Zendesk, Intercom, Notion, Linear — don't
  claim these.)

  2. The Memory Layer — "It never forgets, and knowledge outlives the person"

  This is your differentiator. Noise indexes your files (Drive/Dropbox), analyzes email attachments, and embeds every
  conversation into a searchable brain.

  - Semantic memory (pgvector): ask in plain English, get real answers grounded in your actual messages and documents
  - Document resolution: say "the Acme MSA" or "the payroll spreadsheet" and Noise ranks the real files by confidence —
  no guessing
  - Knowledge graph: tracks which companies, people, and topics matter and who's discussing what
  - Investigation engine: "find every contract expiring in 30 days" → real aggregated answers, not a file dump
  - The moat line: Human knowledge stays with the company — never lost when someone leaves.

  3. The Intelligence Layer — "It tells you what's at risk before you'd notice"

  Deterministic, explainable, not a black box.

  - Relationship health scoring (0–100) from recency, volume, reciprocity, sentiment + business signals (overdue
  invoices, stalled deals, slipping commitments)
  - Relationship state engine: at risk · heating up · waiting on you · cooling down
  - Forecasting: projects relationship health 14–30 days forward, predicts churn risk before it happens
  - Key-person / bus-factor risk: "one employee is the single point of failure for $X in revenue"
  - Executive brief & founder brief: your 60-second morning read — what changed, what's at risk, what to do today
  - Learns your voice: drafts replies in your tone, learned from how you actually write

  ▎ "A few minutes to your first executive brief."

  Onboarding principle to sell: "Connect once. The brain builds itself." No manual setup, no rules, no tagging. The
  value (first brief) lands in under a minute.
